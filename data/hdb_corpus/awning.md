# Awning — HDB Renovation Guidelines for Building Works

| **Types of Building Work** | **Permit Required?** | **Subject to the Following Guidelines/ Conditions** |
|---|---|---|
| Installation of retractable canvas awning up to single storey height within balcony of double storey executive maisonette | No | - Awning is to be fixed to the upper floor beam structure - Fixing onto refuse chute wall is not allowed - Retractable awning should not protrude beyond the balcony |
| Installation of internal metal roller shutter at window or single storey balcony opening. This is not applicable for balconies where window installation is not allowed. | No | Installation must not be fixed onto refuse chute wall |
| Installation of roller shutter at double storey balcony. This is not applicable for loft units. | No | |
| Installation of awning at open-to-sky balconies of topmost unit | Yes | - Awning must be fixed directly above the frame of the full height sliding glass door and at a maximum height of 2.8 metres from the finished floor level - Awning should remain open sided as viewed from the external façade - Awning can be either a fixed awning using lightweight non-combustible material, or a retractable awning and the horizontal projection of the awning shall be kept within the footprint of the balcony - Where both the awning and its supporting structure are of combustible material, the materials shall comply with the Fire Code - Where there are awnings installed adjacent to each other (serving different residential units), there shall be either a separation distance of at least 3 metres between the awnings or separated by an existing fire rated wall which must be higher than the height of the awning - The design of the awning must blend in with the existing façade. The colour of the awning should be neutral colours, specifically light grey, mid grey or off-white - SCDF submission is not required for awning with horizontal projection not exceeding 1.4m and no enclosure at any of the sides. Only need Qualified Person (QP) endorsed plans complying with the Fire Code - For awnings with more than 1.4m horizontal projection and within the footprint of the balcony, flat owners are to engage a Qualified Person (QP) and/or Professional Engineer [PE(Civil)] to design the awning such that it complies with the requirements from relevant authorities (e.g. BCA, URA, SCDF). SCDF’s Plan Approval and Fire Safety Certificate will have to be obtained before submission to HDB for final approval - As the awnings are installed at the exterior of the flat, flat owners and the subsequent buyers have to undertake to maintain the awning and engage a Professional Engineer [PE(Civil)] to carry out a 5-yearly inspection to certify that the awnings remain safe - Flat owners are responsible for the maintenance and cleaning of the awning to prevent stagnant water and litter from collecting on top, in case of mosquito breeding - Flat owner is required to seek Town Council’s clearance for such installation |
| Installation of awning at open-to-sky balconies at intermediate floor units | Yes | - Awning must be fixed directly above the frame of the full height sliding glass door and at a maximum height of 2.8 metres from the finished floor level - Awning should remain open sided as viewed from the external façade - SCDF submission is not required for awning with horizontal projection not exceeding 1.4m and no enclosure at any of the sides. Only need Qualified Person (QP) endorsed plans complying with the Fire Code - Where both the awning and its supporting structure are of light weight non-combustible material, the horizontal projection of the awning shall not exceed 2m and shall be kept within the footprint of the balcony - The awning and its supporting structure must be made of lightweight material having at least 1 hour fire rating when the horizontal projection of the awning exceeds 2 metres and still be kept within the footprint of the balcony - Where there are awnings installed adjacent to each other (serving different residential units), there shall be either a separation distance of at least 3 metres between the awnings or separated by an existing fire rated wall which must be higher than the height of the awning - The design of the awning must blend in with the existing façade. The colour of the awning should be neutral colours, specifically light grey, mid grey or off-white - For awnings with more than 1.4 metres horizontal projection and within the footprint of the balcony, flat owners are to engage a Qualified Person (QP) and/or Professional Engineer [PE(Civil)] to design the awning such that it complies with the requirements from relevant authorities (e.g. BCA, URA, SCDF). SCDF’s Plan Approval and Fire Safety Certificate will have to be obtained before submission to HDB for final approval - As the awnings are installed at the exterior of the flat, flat owners and the subsequent buyers must undertake to maintain the awning and engage a Professional Engineer [PE(Civil)] to carry out a 5-yearly inspection to certify that the awnings remain safe - Flat owners are responsible for the maintenance and cleaning of the awning to prevent stagnant water and litter from collecting on top, in case of mosquito breeding - Flat owner is required to seek Town Council’s clearance for such installation |
